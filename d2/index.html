    01 The Problem

    Every year, companies spend millions on brand strategy.
    Twelve months later, most of it is obsolete.

    Brilliance that can't be told can't be sold.

    I've watched billion-dollar technologies die in conference rooms because no one could explain why they mattered. The gap between complexity and story is where most innovation gets buried.

    Vision without architecture is just a daydream.

    Everyone has a "five-year vision." Almost no one has the strategic architecture to survive year two. I build the scaffolding that turns ambitious thinking into executable reality.

    The most advanced technology is invisible until someone gives it a voice.

    Edge AI. Precision instruments. Flow dynamics. These aren't simple products. They need a translator who speaks both engineering and emotion.

    Most consulting delivers a moment of clarity — then entropy takes over. I got tired of watching good strategy decay. So I started building systems that don't just deliver intelligence. They compound it.

    04 Origin

    Process engineer.
    Brand strategist.
    Systems architect.

    I started my career designing process systems for power plants and EV charging infrastructure. I learned something that never left me: the most sophisticated systems in the world fail if the people operating them don't understand why they matter.

    That realization pulled me into brand strategy. For fifteen years, I've worked at the intersection of technology and meaning — helping leaders across semiconductor fabrication, chip design, edge AI, and industrial computing close the gap between what they build and what the world understands.

    The honest part: the first three years were humbling. I came from engineering where problems had solutions. Brand strategy has no right answers — only better questions. I failed spectacularly before I found my footing. That failure taught me more than any success: systems break. What matters is whether they break forward.

    Today, as Creative Intelligence Officer at RedPeak — part of the kyu Collective — I lead strategic engagements across Asia's technology sector. My approach is shaped by an engineer's insistence on systems, a strategist's instinct for narrative, and a musician's ear for resonance.

    When I'm not building brand systems, I play the handpan — a steel instrument where every note resonates with every other. It's not a bad metaphor for how I think about brands.
